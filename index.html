Yoshi Aligina is an American data scientist and software developer currently studying at Rutgers University. Known for combining machine learning techniques with statistical inference to solve complex real-world problems, particularly in the areas of legal text classification and predictive modeling.[1]

Aligina's work focuses on the intersection of computer science, data science, and statistical analysis, with notable contributions to natural language processing and regression modeling projects. Their academic background spans multiple disciplines including computer science, data science, statistics, and philosophy.[2]
